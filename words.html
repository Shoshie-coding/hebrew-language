Common Hebrew Phrases

Shalom (שָׁלוֹם)

Shalom is perhaps the most well known Hebrew word. It means hello, goodbye, and peace. It is also found in the saying Shalom Aleichem (Hebrew: שָׁלוֹם עֲלֵיכֶם‎, 'Peace be upon you') which is also a traditional song sung by Jews every Friday night upon returning home from synagogue prayer on the eve of the Shabbat.

Ken and Lo

Ken means Yes and Lo means No - we can see this in the famous brand Kenvelo – which literally means Yes and No where ve means "and".

Slicha

Slicha means sorry or excuse me.

Toda

Toda means thank you it’s used often in the phrase Toda rabameaning thank you very much.

BeVakasha

Saying BeVakasha is a common and polite way to respond to someone thanking you. When someone says Toda or Toda raba, you can simply reply with BeVakasha. It also means please.
